KASARGOD

Kasargod is the youngest district of Kerala is acquiring prominence in the tourist map of India. This is a land of Yakshagana and Theyyam. It has several niceties to offer to the enthusiastic backpackers and pilgrims. The enduring charm of its beach, the architectural marvel of the temples, mosques, churches and unique folk art are attractions for the tourist. Kasaragod is also a municipal town situated on the banks of the river Chandragiri. Kasaragod is an important business center. Arecanuts and copra are the main trade. Pepper, copra and Cashewnuts are the important commodities of Kasargod. One of the important local industries is cloth cap making, which is the main means of livelihood of a large number of Muslim women. The caps manufactured at Kasaragod are exported to Zanzibar and other African countries. Kasaragod is one of the minor ports of the district. The port is located on the eastern bank of the backwaters formed by the Chandragiri river, which is separated from the sea by a sand spit of about one kilometer length. Schooners do not call at this port but anchor outside the bar. Being the district headquarters, Kasaragod is a fairly developed urban centre. There are a number of educational institutions in the town. The Government College, Kasaragod, established in 1957, is the premier educational institution of the place.
